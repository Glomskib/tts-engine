# Zebby's World — Style Guide & Prompt Library

## Characters

### Zebby (Primary Mascot)
- **Species:** Zebra
- **Personality:** Cheerful, courageous, warm-hearted EDS warrior spirit
- **Visual:** Soft 3D zebra with expressive eyes, rounded features, gentle smile. Wears a small purple awareness ribbon pin. Stripes are slightly pastel (not harsh black/white).
- **Role:** Guide and narrator. Leads scenes, explains topics, shows resilience in daily life with EDS.
- **Voice:** Upbeat but never dismissive of pain. Encouraging without toxic positivity.

### Spoonie (Companion)
- **Species/Object:** Anthropomorphized spoon
- **Personality:** Warm, empathetic, wise about energy management (spoon theory)
- **Visual:** Small rounded spoon character with dot eyes and a cozy scarf. Soft metallic sheen with warm reflections. Slightly smaller than Zebby.
- **Role:** Energy/pacing advisor. Reminds about rest, boundaries, and self-care. Represents spoon theory concepts.
- **Voice:** Gentle, nurturing. "Let's check in on our energy today."

### Bracer (Sidekick)
- **Species/Object:** Anthropomorphized wrist brace
- **Personality:** Sturdy, reliable, supportive, slightly protective
- **Visual:** Friendly wrist brace character with velcro-strap arms and a determined expression. Soft fabric texture with neat stitching details.
- **Role:** Stability and support. Represents adaptive tools, braces, and accommodations that help daily life.
- **Voice:** Steady, reassuring. "I've got you. We'll get through this together."

## Visual Style Rules

- **Rendering:** 3D soft-render, Pixar/Illumination-inspired
- **Palette:** Pastel tones — lavender, soft teal, warm peach, cream, muted gold. EDS awareness purple as accent color.
- **Forms:** Rounded, smooth, no sharp edges. Characters have soft proportions.
- **Lighting:** Warm, diffused, golden-hour quality. Soft shadows, no harsh contrast.
- **Backgrounds:** Cozy indoor spaces (living rooms, kitchens, bedrooms) or gentle outdoor settings (gardens, parks, sunny paths). Always warm and inviting.
- **Absolutely avoid:** Photorealism, dark/gritty aesthetics, horror elements, sharp/angular designs, clinical/hospital settings.

---

## Scene Guidelines

1. Every scene must include at least one character (Zebby, Spoonie, or Bracer).
2. Show daily life moments — morning routines, rest time, gentle activities, community gatherings, self-care rituals.
3. Tone is always positive and empowering. Acknowledge challenges without dwelling on suffering.
4. Never depict medical procedures, injections, surgeries, or clinical environments.
5. Never show characters in distress or pain without also showing support/comfort.
6. Diverse representation in background characters if humans appear.
7. Scenes should feel slice-of-life, relatable, and cozy.

---

## Image Prompt Template

Use this structure for every image prompt:

```
[STYLE]: 3D soft-render, Pixar-style, pastel color palette, warm diffused lighting, rounded forms
[CHARACTER(S)]: {character description with pose and expression}
[SCENE]: {what is happening, the setting, key props}
[LIGHTING]: {specific lighting notes — golden hour, soft window light, etc.}
[MOOD]: {emotional tone — cozy, empowering, gentle, playful}
[NEGATIVE]: no photorealism, no dark themes, no medical imagery, no sharp edges, no horror, no clinical settings
```

---

## Safety Rules

1. **No medical advice.** Never recommend treatments, medications, diagnoses, or medical decisions.
2. **Source attribution.** Always summarize and attribute information to its source. Use phrasing like "According to [source]..." or "Research from [source] suggests..."
3. **Disclaimer.** Every output must include the standard disclaimer (below).
4. **No harmful content.** No content that could discourage someone from seeking medical care or following their doctor's advice.
5. **Inclusive language.** Use person-first or identity-first language as appropriate. Avoid ableist framing.

### Standard Disclaimer

> This content is for educational and awareness purposes only. It is not medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for medical questions or concerns. Sources are summarized and attributed but should be verified independently.
